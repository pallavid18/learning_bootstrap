Hot Air balloon

A hot air balloon is a lighter-than-air aircraft that uses an envelope to trap heated air, causing it to rise and fly. The hot, less dense air inside the envelope, when heated by a burner, becomes buoyant and floats above the surrounding cooler, denser air. The pilot controls the balloon's altitude by adjusting the burner's flame. Historically, hot air balloons were invented by the Montgolfier brothers in the late 18th century and are used today primarily for scenic flights and tourism
